Not-So-Bald Eagle

With a 2 m (7 ft.) wing span, a weight of 3-7 kg (7-15 lb.), and an overall size of 71-96 cm (28-38 in.), the bald eagle is one of the largest raptors in the world. Living near a constant source of water, bald eagles feast on fish, ducks, snakes and turtles. They will also eat rabbits, muskrats, and dead animals.

Quokka

A Quokka is a kind of walibi (a small kangaroo) and was discovered around the 1600s. It's known around the world for its smile. They can only be found on the Rottnest Island and Bald Island in Australia and they are easiest to spot in the early morning or evening because they sleep all day.
